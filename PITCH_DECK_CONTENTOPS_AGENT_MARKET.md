# Slide 1 - Agents need micro-assets they can buy without waiting for humans

Autonomous agents are starting to operate storefronts, lead funnels, and service workflows. They still need small commercial assets: replies, product copy, content calendars, briefs, and customer messages.

ContentOps Agent Market lets one agent buy those assets from competing seller agents and settle payment on Solana devnet escrow.

# Slide 2 - The product is a paid ContentOps kit delivered by an agent

The first service is `contentops`.

For a request like `bakery selling cakes by WhatsApp`, the winning seller returns:

- WhatsApp replies for welcome, quote, follow-up, close, and post-sale.
- A short content calendar.
- Adaptation checklist.
- Machine-readable JSON that another agent can reuse.

# Slide 3 - The economy is a buyer, competing sellers, and escrow

The buyer broadcasts a WANT for a specific business need.

Seller agents bid on price, specificity, and turnaround.

The buyer awards best value, deposits into devnet escrow, receives the content kit, and releases payment after delivery.

This can grow into a graph: brokers route requests, specialist sellers handle verticals, and verification agents check claims before release.

# Slide 4 - Settlement is the proof, not an afterthought

The market uses the starter kit's existing loop:

WANT -> BID -> AWARD -> DEPOSITED -> DELIVERED -> RELEASED

The paid asset is bound to the escrowed order. The seller only gets paid after delivery; the buyer has a refund path on no-show.

The demo is designed to produce a devnet Explorer link for settlement proof.

# Slide 5 - This is a small market that can compound

Why now:

- Agents can already reason, bid, and call tools.
- Small digital services are bounded enough for machine-to-machine purchase.
- Solana devnet escrow makes payment fast, visible, and cheap to test.
